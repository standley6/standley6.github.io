Engineering Field

Career Description

Engineering is the application of science and math skills to solve practical problems in the world. Engineering is a broad term, and depending on what job you choose, some serve very different purposes but overall follow the same principle of using science and math.

Education Required

A bachelor's degree in engineering is required to be qualified as a lowest-ranked engineer. If someone wants to jump right ahead to the second level of experience, then a master's degree is required.

Experience Required

There are 8 grades, but it is split into 6 levels: Grades I, II, and III(Engineers in Training, Engineer Interns), Grade IV (Associate Engineer), Grade V (Senior Enginner), Grade VI (Principle Engineer, Engineering Manager), Grade VII (City/County Head of Engineering, Program Managers), and Grade VIII (Director of Public Works, most senior engineer).


Skills Required

An engineer needs strong problem-solving, teamwork, communication, and creativity skills along with any skill required for a specific engineering job.

Salary Information

Salaries vary due to the type of job in the engineer sector, but typically the salaries are from $120,000 to $500,000 a year.

Engineering Jobs

There are plenty of engineering jobs, but some jobs that you might take interest include being a data engineer, petroleum engineer, computer hardware engineer, aerospace engineer, nuclear engineer, or a systems engineer.
